## Positioning summary

Inspire OPs is a **back-office, vertical** product: commission reconciliation and
agent-payment transparency for **transit insurance agencies**. It is not a horizontal
CRM or a front-office sales/quoting tool. Most "insurtech" products we'll be compared
to actually solve a *different* problem and are complementary rather than head-to-head.

## Gail (meetgail.com)

> Site blocked automated fetch; details below are from general knowledge and must be
> verified before external use.

- **What it is:** AI "front-office employee" for P&C insurance agencies — answers
  inbound calls, quotes new business, services policies, follows up on leads.
- **Scope:** Broad, horizontal, customer-facing (revenue generation + service).
- **Maturity:** Funded, marketed, multi-agency, active paid go-to-market.

### Inspire OPs vs. Gail

| Dimension | Inspire OPs | Gail |
|---|---|---|
| Part of agency | Back office (commissions, payouts) | Front office (calls, quoting, service) |
| Problem | Commission-float, agent mistrust, owed-vs-paid visibility | Customer service + sales automation |
| Specialization | Deep vertical (transit insurance commission workflow) | Broad horizontal AI assistant |
| Maturity | 2 clients, just deployed | Funded, marketed, multi-agency |
| Relationship | **Complementary** — could run side by side | Not a direct competitor today |

### Where Inspire is stronger
- Purpose-built commission engine: carrier statement ingest, expected-vs-actual
  reconciliation, exception flagging, automated agent payout statements.
- Transit-insurance domain depth (upfront-on-first-sale, agency-retained renewals,
  the 30+15 day float) — hard to copy.
- Trust/transparency: agents see exactly what they're owed and when — an agent-retention
  lever a quoting bot doesn't address.

### Where Gail is ahead
- Company maturity: team, brand, funding, sales engine.
- Broader scope and a flashier AI/voice demo story.
- Real go-to-market motion (paid ads) vs. our 2-client footprint.

### Strategic takeaway
Position **next to** Gail, not against it (front office + back office). The real risk is
not Gail winning the commission niche but (a) an unknown back-office competitor, or
(b) Gail expanding into commissions later. Moat = depth in the commission/transit niche;
widen it by converting the two clients to signed, paying contracts and turning their
results into proof.
